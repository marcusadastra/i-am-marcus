What is Latin?

Latin is way more than just some dead language from history books. It’s the foundation for tons of modern languages like Spanish, French, and Italian.

The Romans spread it far and wide, and you can still see its legacy everywhere today, even in English, where half our words come from Latin roots. When you start learning Latin, it feels like unlocking this secret superpower—suddenly, you see connections and understand words on a deeper level. It’s not just vocabulary; it’s how the Romans shaped so much of our world, from laws and government to philosophy and literature.

What’s wild is that Latin lets you read the greatest works ever written, like Virgil or Ovid, in their original form. It’s like having a one-on-one connection with people from thousands of years ago. And yeah, the grammar is tough, but that’s what makes it rewarding. It’s like solving a giant puzzle that makes you think harder and appreciate the structure of language itself. So, if you’re into history, languages, or how humans think, Latin is like this key that opens up a world you never knew existed.

The idea behind this project was to make language learning less about memorizing and more about exploring.

I developed an app that uses Augmented Reality (AR) to bring Latin and Spanish to life. It lets users see objects in the real world and hear that vocab pronounced in either Latin or Spanish.
