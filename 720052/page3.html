What is the Shewb cube?

October 21, 2022
The Skewb is a unique twisty puzzle that challenges solvers with its diagonal rotation mechanism, setting it apart from the traditional Rubik’s Cube. Instead of rotating along flat faces, the Skewb pivots around its corners, making it feel more like a pyramid in motion. While it retains the cube shape with six faces and eight corners, the puzzle’s movements are less intuitive, requiring solvers to think in terms of diagonal slices. With only four corner and four central pieces per face, the Skewb might seem simpler than a 3x3 cube, but its unconventional mechanics demand a different approach and specific algorithms. Its combination of simplicity and complexity has made the Skewb a popular puzzle in speedcubing competitions, where solvers aim for lightning-fast solutions.

What is the Square-1 Cube? See this demo

January 07, 2023
The Square-1 is a fascinating twisty puzzle that stands out due to its ability to change shape as it is twisted, unlike the traditional Rubik’s Cube that maintains its cube form throughout solving. It consists of three layers—two outer layers with wedge-shaped pieces and a middle layer made of two halves. As you rotate the middle layer 180 degrees, the top and bottom layers can rotate independently, causing the puzzle to scramble into irregular, non-cube shapes. This shape-shifting aspect adds a unique challenge, as solvers must not only align the colors but also return the puzzle to its original cube form before finishing the solve. The puzzle's unconventional structure and movement make it more complex than many other twisty puzzles. Popular in speedcubing competitions, the Square-1 requires both spatial awareness and a deep understanding of algorithms, offering a thrilling experience for enthusiasts who enjoy puzzles with an extra layer of difficulty.
